The World Health Organization reports that Iron deficiency is the most common global nutrition deficiency — in both developing as well as industrialized nations.

An estimated 2 billion people suffer from persistent iron deficiency — resulting in fatigue, increased susceptibility to illness as well as impaired memory and motor performance. Similarly, poor birth outcomes, including prematurity and low birth weight have been associated with anemia during pregnancy. Poor iron bioavailability of dietary iron is cited as the primary causal factor in people with low iron status.

Often, health professionals will prescribe supplementary iron (in the form of capsules, tablets, sachets or syrups) containing harsh iron salts to treat iron deficiency anemia and low serum ferritin. However, compliance with these recommendations is often poor due to the adverse effects associated with most commercial iron products. These include gastrointestinal upset, nausea, and bloating.
