In July 2007, a search of the NCBI (National Center for Biotechnology Information, Washington D.C.) PubMed database using the search string 'metabolic syndrome' returns over 20,000 articles. Limiting the search to articles published before 1970 returns less than 500 articles. As of mid-2014, a similar search returns over 50,000 published articles. Clearly, there is a growing interest in – and awareness of – this constellation of clinical symptoms understood to precede the onset of type 2 diabetes, which is a chronic condition of abnormal glucose metabolism.

According to the National Cholesterol Education Program Criteria - Adult Treatment Panel III guidelines (NCEP-ATPIII), the clinical definition of Metabolic Syndrome includes three or more of the following five conditions present in the patient1:

  • Waist Circumference >102 cm (men) or 88 cm (women)
  • Fasting Serum Triacylglycerol concentration >1.7 mmol/L (>150 mg/dL)
  • Fasting HDL cholesterol concentration <1.0 mmol/L (<45 men/<50 women mg/dL).
  • Impaired fasting glucose. Plasma glucose between 6.1 and 6.9 mmol/L (>110 mg dL)
  • Blood pressure >135/85 mm Hg or use of blood pressure medication.

The World Health Organization lists similar clinical criteria for Metabolic Syndrome but expands the list of possible clinical findings to include insulin resistance (including type 2 diabetes), BMI >30 and/or waist:hip ratio of >0.9 in men or >0.85 in women, excess urinary albumin excretion (>20 mcg/min) or albuimin:creatinine ratio >30 mg/g).

Metabolic Syndrome is not restricted primarily to the United States. Many European countries report growing public health concern regarding obesity and diabetes, and Wang et al. (2007) estimate that the prevalence of metabolic syndrome among Chinese adults at 15% - amounting to approximately 180 million people. This group is composed primarily of the middle and upper-class urban beneficiaries of China’s tremendous economic growth over the past few decades. The justification for a quality product that can serve as an aid in overcoming the metabolic syndrome is clear.
